Election Info

Vote on Tuesday 11/6 from 7am - 8pm

No one is required to show photo ID to vote in this election

Voter ID

Due to Pennsylvania's Voter ID Law, voters will be asked, but not required, to show an acceptable photo ID on Election Day, 11/6.

If it is your first time voting at your polling place, be sure to check what you will need to bring with you in the next section.

First-Time Voters

All voters who appear at a polling place for the first time must show proof of identification (not necessarily photo ID), such as:
  • Pennsylvania driver's license or PennDOT ID card
  • ID issued by any Commonwealth agency
  • ID issued by the U.S. Government
  • U.S. passport
  • U.S. Armed Forces ID
  • Student ID
  • Employee ID
  • Non-photo ID issued by the Commonwealth
  • Non-photo ID issued by the U.S. Government
  • Firearm permit
  • Current utility bill
  • Current bank statement
  • Current paycheck
  • Government check

More Information

For more information on the election, contact the Office of City Commissioner Stephanie Singer at 215-686-3460 or 215-686-3461.
